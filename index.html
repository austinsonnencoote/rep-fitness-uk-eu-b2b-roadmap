Use Case 01 — UK B2B Portal & Sign-Up
UK B2B Portal & Commercial Sign-Up

A unified commercial layer for REP Fitness UK/EU: existing UK accounts get a self-serve reorder portal, and new commercial buyers apply through a purpose-built form that routes directly into Shopify and HubSpot. Both are built on the same foundation — UK first, designed to scale across every EU market after.

The Goal

Give existing UK commercial customers a front-end portal where they can browse products by collection (weights, cardio, benches, etc.), see their pre-negotiated B2B pricing in GBP, and reorder without a rep involved. V1 launches in the UK. The architecture is set up from day one so adding DE, FR, IT and ES markets is additive — not a rebuild.

How to Build It
1
Enable B2B and Create Your UK Market

In Shopify Admin, activate B2B under Settings → Customer accounts. Create a UK Market in Markets — this becomes the container for your UK B2B catalog, currency (GBP), VAT rules, and domain. Every future EU geo (DE, FR, IT, ES) gets its own Market added on top of this same foundation.

Markets → Add Market → United Kingdom · Currency: GBP · Domain: repfitness.co.uk
2
Build Your UK B2B Catalog and Price Lists

Create a B2B-specific catalog in the Shopify admin — separate from DTC. Assign the catalog to the UK Market. Set up price lists within the catalog: fixed B2B pricing in GBP, or % off retail per product or collection. B2B pricing only surfaces on login — DTC visitors always see DTC pricing.

Catalogs → Create Catalog → Assign to UK Market → Add Price List (GBP) → Scope to collections (Weights, Cardio, Benches)
3
Create Companies and Assign to UK Market

Each existing UK commercial account becomes a Company in Shopify. The Company record holds: contacts, locations, assigned catalog, payment terms, and order history. Companies are scoped to the UK Market — when you add a DE or FR Market later, those companies go there. No overlap, no manual filtering.

B2B → Companies → New Company → Assign Catalog + UK Market → Add Contacts + Locations
4
Set Up B2B Shipping Rules (Different from DTC)

B2B shipping profiles can be completely separate from DTC. Create a dedicated shipping profile for UK B2B orders: pallet/LTL carrier rates (DPD, Tuffnells, Palletways), freight options for large orders, and Net terms-compatible shipping. Assign the profile to your B2B catalog — DTC shipping is untouched.

Settings → Shipping → Create Profile → Assign to UK B2B Catalog → Set pallet/freight options for commercial orders
5
Configure Net Payment Terms & VAT Handling

Assign Net 30 (or Net 60) terms at the Company level — each account gets the terms you negotiated. Shopify Markets automatically applies UK VAT (20%) on orders, with VAT-inclusive or VAT-exclusive display configured per market. When a B2B customer checks out, the order is placed with a due date automatically set. Shopify Flow handles reminders and auto-charge when the date hits — no manual AR chasing.

Company record → Payment Terms → Net 30 / Net 60 → Markets → VAT settings → Shopify Flow: auto-charge trigger on due date
6
Build the Front End — Collections Browse + Quick Buy

Your theme controls the B2B portal experience. Enable collection-based browsing so commercial buyers can navigate by product type. Add a Quick Buy / Quick Order component so frequent buyers can drop quantities directly from the collection page without clicking into each PDP. V1 can use an off-the-shelf theme component — no custom dev required.

Theme editor → B2B Customer Account portal → Enable Collections nav → Add Quick Order List section (available in most Plus themes)
Scaling Across EU — The Markets Playbook

You build the infrastructure once. Every new EU geo is a configuration layer on top.

When REP UK is ready to open the B2B portal to German, French, Italian or Spanish buyers: add a DE Market, create a DE-specific price list (EUR), assign a DE catalog (same products, different pricing/VAT handling), and assign DE companies to that market. The portal front end, checkout logic, and order management are already built — you're just extending to a new lane, not rebuilding the road.

Add DE/FR/IT/ES Markets → EUR currency
Duplicate UK catalog → Adjust EU pricing
Shopify Markets: EU VAT auto-applied per country
New EU companies → Assigned to country Market
Same theme, same portal UX
Key Features Involved
B2B Catalogs

Scoped Product & Pricing Visibility

Each catalog controls which products a company sees and at what price. DTC visitors never see B2B pricing. Multiple catalogs can run simultaneously — UK commercial, DE commercial, FR commercial — each with different terms.

Shopify Markets

One Store, Multiple Geos

Markets controls currency, language, domain, VAT rules, and which catalog applies — per geography. Adding a new EU country is additive. No separate Shopify store, no duplicated theme work, no split admin.

Net Payment Terms

AR Built Into the Order Flow

Net 30/60 set per company. Due dates attached to every order automatically. Shopify Flow handles dunning reminders and auto-charge — eliminating the manual AR chase and the unmatched BACS/SEPA wire problem.

Commercial Sign-Up Flow
Application Form & CRM Routing

A purpose-built application form for UK commercial buyers — collected via Shopify Forms, automatically creating an unapproved company record, and notifying the sales team in HubSpot so they can vet and activate accounts without any manual data entry.

The Goal

UK commercial buyers who want a B2B account submit a form on repfitness.co.uk. That form automatically creates a Company record in Shopify with their details, set to unapproved. The sales team sees the lead in HubSpot and either activates or declines. Once approved, the buyer gets access to the portal — no rep data entry, no email threads.

How to Build It
1
Build the Application Form with Shopify Forms

Install the Shopify Forms app (free, native). Build a custom form collecting: business name, contact name, email, phone, business type (gym, corporate, institutional), number of locations, and any UK commercial-specific fields REP needs (e.g., VAT number, Companies House registration number). Embed it on a dedicated /commercial or /wholesale page on repfitness.co.uk.

Shopify Forms → Create Form → Custom fields (incl. VAT no. + Companies House no.) → Embed on /commercial page
2
Auto-Create the Company Record on Submission

Use Shopify Flow to trigger on form submission: create a Company record in B2B with the submitted details, scope it to the UK Market, set status to Unapproved, and tag it with source (e.g., "Web Application — UK"). The company exists in the system immediately — the sales team doesn't need to create it manually when they go to approve.

Shopify Flow → Trigger: Form Submitted → Action: Create Company (UK Market, status: Unapproved) → Tag: Web-Application-UK
3
Push the Lead to HubSpot via Shopify Flow API Action

Immediately after the Company record is created, Shopify Flow fires an HTTP POST to HubSpot's Contacts API — no middleware, no manual export. Flow's Send HTTP Request action maps the form fields (business name, email, phone, VAT number, company size) directly to HubSpot contact and deal properties. The UK sales team gets a new lead in their pipeline the moment the form is submitted, assigned and ready to work.

Shopify Flow → Send HTTP Request
URL: https://api.hubapi.com/crm/v3/objects/contacts
Method: POST
Header: Authorization: Bearer {{hubspot_private_app_token}}
Body:
{
"properties": {
"email": "{{form.email}}",
"company": "{{form.business_name}}",
"phone": "{{form.phone}}",
"vat_number": "{{form.vat_number}}",
"hs_lead_status": "NEW",
"lead_source": "REP UK Shopify B2B Form"
}
}

This creates a new Contact in HubSpot immediately. From there, a HubSpot workflow can automatically assign the lead to the right UK sales rep, create a Deal in the B2B pipeline, and send a Slack or email notification — all without anyone touching Shopify admin. REP's UK sales team works the lead in the system they already use.

Flow Trigger: Form Submitted → HTTP POST to HubSpot Contacts API → HubSpot Workflow: Assign UK Rep + Create Deal + Notify Team
4
Sales Team Vets and Activates

The rep clicks into HubSpot, reviews the lead, and either activates the company (changing status to Approved in Shopify, assigning the UK catalog and Net payment terms) or declines. Once approved, the buyer receives an invitation email to set up their account and access the portal — the whole flow is automated from form submission to active account.

Shopify Admin → B2B → Companies → Approve → Assign UK Catalog + Net Terms → Auto-send invite email
Scaling This Across EU

Add a market-specific version of the form — same flow, different routing.

When the DE portal launches, create a /commercial-de form variant collecting EUR and DE-specific business details (USt-IdNr.). Flow routes DE submissions to DE company records (assigned to the DE Market). HubSpot gets the same notification — the sales team handles DE leads exactly as they handle UK ones. No new tooling, no new processes.

Duplicate form → DE/FR/IT/ES versions
Flow routes to country Market companies
Same HubSpot pipeline
Country catalog auto-assigned on approval
Key Features Involved
Shopify Forms

Native, No-Code Form Builder

Free app, native to Shopify. Builds fully customizable forms that embed anywhere on the storefront. Submissions feed directly into Shopify Flow for automation — no third-party form tool needed.

Shopify Flow

The Automation Layer

Triggers on form submission to create company records, set approval status, assign catalogs, and send buyer invite emails. Critically: the Send HTTP Request action fires a direct API call to HubSpot the moment a wholesale request comes in — no middleware, no manual data entry, no export. The lead lands in HubSpot before a rep could even open Shopify admin.

HubSpot Integration

Leads Where the Team Already Works

The native HubSpot + Shopify integration syncs company and customer data bidirectionally. New UK B2B applications surface in HubSpot as deals — with all form fields mapped to the contact record.
